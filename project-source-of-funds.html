Cross-Chain Source of Funds Tracing

Tracing digital asset movement across Solana and Ethereum bridge infrastructure to determine the earliest identifiable funding source behind exchange-linked deposits.

Objective

To determine the origin of funds for transactions that ultimately deposited into exchange wallets by tracing the transaction path backward through bridge infrastructure and intermediary wallets.

Tooling Used

Etherscan · Arbiscan · Solscan · Arkham Intelligence · Wormhole Explorer · Manual transaction analysis

Methodology

  • Identified final recipient deposit addresses linked to exchange infrastructure.
  • Traced transaction flow backward through Across Protocol and Wormhole bridge.
  • Analyzed intermediary wallets across both Ethereum and Solana networks.
  • Verified transaction continuity using timestamp alignment and value matching.
  • Confirmed end-to-end fund flow from source to final deposit destination.

Key Findings

The transaction flow demonstrated a continuous and traceable path from a Binance hot wallet, through multiple intermediary wallets on Solana, into the Wormhole bridge, then across Ethereum via Across Protocol, and finally into identified KuCoin deposit addresses.

Evidence Basis

The conclusion was supported by consistent transaction flow, matching transfer values across each stage, aligned timestamps, repeated interaction patterns with bridge protocols, and final transfers into known exchange deposit addresses.

Outcome & Relevance

This case demonstrates the ability to trace source of funds across multiple blockchain networks and bridge infrastructure, supporting AML investigations, transaction monitoring processes, and compliance validation in complex digital asset environments.
