Executive Overview

This case study documents a structured blockchain investigation in which funds were traced backward from KuCoin-linked destination wallets through intermediary recipient wallets, Across Protocol bridge activity, and Wormhole bridge interaction to an upstream source path beginning from a Binance hot wallet.

The analytical objective was to determine whether the destination deposits represented isolated transfers or were part of a continuous, verifiable, cross-chain flow. The evidence supports the latter conclusion.

Primary Finding End-to-End Traceability

The fund flow remains visible from upstream funding through bridge infrastructure into exchange-linked deposits without a break in the traced route.

Analytical Outcome Coordinated Routing Pattern

The behavior observed across bridges, receiver wallets, and deposits aligns with structured routing rather than unrelated wallet activity.

Methodology

The case was built using backward tracing, post-bridge recipient analysis, final deposit grouping, and transaction-level consistency checks across multiple blockchain stages.

1

Backward Tracing

Deposits were traced back through intermediary Ethereum wallets, Across Protocol, and Wormhole to locate the last visible upstream funding path.

2

Bridge Interaction Review

Bridge recipient and routing behavior were assessed to confirm that funds entered, moved through, and exited bridge infrastructure in a coherent sequence.

3

Correlation Analysis

Timestamps, amounts, and wallet transitions were compared across each stage to test whether the same funds could be followed continuously.

4

Exchange Attribution

Repeated destination addresses were grouped and interpreted as KuCoin-linked deposit endpoints based on final transfer behavior and destination reuse.

Risk Flags

The following analytical flags are relevant from an AML, transaction monitoring, and blockchain investigation perspective.

Medium

Cross-Chain Routing

Movement across Wormhole and Across Protocol increases tracing complexity and may indicate an attempt to fragment visibility across networks.

High

Exchange Deposit Convergence

Final convergence into exchange-linked deposit wallets is a key risk indicator because it can signal an attempt to cash out, consolidate, or further layer funds.

Medium

Structured Intermediary Distribution

Use of multiple recipient and intermediary wallets before final deposit may reflect deliberate routing intended to complicate straightforward attribution.

Medium

Rapid Timing Between Stages

Short intervals between bridge execution and final deposits support continuity, but also suggest coordinated execution rather than casual user behavior.

Low

Strong Trace Continuity

From an evidentiary standpoint, the case benefits from clear continuity, which reduces analytical uncertainty even though it does not remove behavioral risk.

Medium

Repeated Destination Usage

Reuse of the same final deposit addresses across multiple routed transfers reinforces exchange attribution and suggests operational consistency.

Conclusion

The investigation supports a coherent end-to-end linkage between the upstream funding source, the bridge infrastructure, intermediary recipient wallets, and the final KuCoin-linked deposit addresses.

The case is strengthened by four key evidentiary pillars: uninterrupted fund flow, close timing alignment, value consistency, and common source attribution. These factors collectively support the conclusion that the observed deposits are part of a continuous routed path rather than unrelated blockchain events.

Analytical Conclusion

Based on the reconstructed path and supporting transaction behavior, the final deposit wallets can be reasonably linked to the traced source flow through cross-chain infrastructure and intermediary routing. This makes the case suitable as a portfolio example of practical blockchain investigation, transaction monitoring, and AML-focused analysis.
